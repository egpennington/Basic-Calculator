About Accounting Error Calculator

Are your debit and credits off?

Small accounting mistakes can cost your business time and money. Bad data alone costs U.S. businesses more than $3.1 trillion a year, according to a report from IBM.


According to another report, the average employee makes 118 mistakes a year. Automated systems can help accountants detect mistakes before they require costly intervention. The Accounting Error Calculator can help detect two big accounting errors and save time


Error 1: Transposition errors

A transposition error describes an event where an accountant accidentally reverses two adjacent digits, when recording transactions. Although the error may seem small in scale, it often results in substantial financial incongruities that can have a great impact in other areas.


Error 2: Debit/Credit reversals

If the difference is divisible by 2 you may have transferred a debit-balanced account to the trial balance as a credit or a credit-balanced account as a debit. When the difference is divisible by 2, look for an amount in the trial balance that is equal to one-half of the difference. Therefore if the difference is $800 look for an account with a balance of $400 and verify if it is in the wrong column.
